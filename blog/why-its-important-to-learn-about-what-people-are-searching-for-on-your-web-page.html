Why Is It Important to Learn About Page Searches

It can be daunting to learn that people landed on your website are unable to find the information they need. It hurts conversion, increases bounce rate and puts your ad money to waste. Yet worst, I learned that people who couldn’t find the information they are looking for seldom come back in the short run. Unfortunately, it’s really hard to aggregate what people are looking for after they land on your webpage.

Personally, I use CTRL F to search on the pages I visit a lot to locate useful information quickly. For example, I was recently looking for an avalanche backpack for my backcountry ski trips. It usually comes in separate units -- the backpack itself and the airbag. Most manufacturers sell them separately. And you will be surprised how difficult it was to find out if the backpack comes with the airbag unit. I ended up using page search and quickly ruled out the sellers if the page doesn’t contain the “airbag” keyword. And I looked back, and just realized just how much I searched on the page. When I am trying to purchase a piece of furniture, or pricing when I am browsing a SaaS product website.

We are consumed by the rich information and I appreciate the websites that show me what I am looking for prominently. As a blogger and a partime helper of my wife’s online shop, I realized just how much we might have been missing out on conversion when certain keywords are missing or too hidden on the page. Me bouncing from those online ski shops because I couldn’t find the “airbag” keyword is a perfect example.

So I built CTRL-F, a tool that captures the keyword that people search for on my page for my blog and my wife’s online shop. It has served us well so far. It helped us identify what people are looking for on the page so we could reorganize the content. And it has also given me new ideas of blog articles.
